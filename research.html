Research

MRI-based measurement, membrane processes, and computational modeling.

Research Interests

My work sits at the intersection of experimental measurement and computational modeling in process engineering. I'm interested in understanding transport phenomena inside complex systems — particularly membrane modules — using non-invasive imaging and simulation.

MRI-based Flow & Concentration Measurement

Phase-contrast MRI velocimetry and relaxometry for spatially resolved measurement of velocity fields and solute concentrations in membrane systems. Working with Siemens Magnetom Skyra, custom pulse sequences, and MATLAB-based image reconstruction.

Membrane Processes

Electrodialysis, sterile filtration, and single-use intravenous in-line filters. Focus on flow distribution, fouling, and process optimization through a combination of experiment and simulation.

CFD Simulation

COMSOL Multiphysics modeling of flow and mass transport in membrane modules. Navier-Stokes coupled with Darcy's law, mesh optimization for thin-layer geometries, and validation against MRI data.

Bayesian Optimization & Statistical Methods

Applying Bayesian optimization and design-of-experiments methods to efficiently explore process parameter spaces. Statistical quantification of flow maldistribution using Coefficient of Variation and related metrics.
