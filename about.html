Hello!

I'm Simon, a process engineer with a PhD from RWTH Aachen University, where I worked at the Chair of Chemical Process Engineering (AVT.CVT). My research sits at the intersection of measurement science and process engineering — I use MRI to look inside membrane systems, build CFD models to understand what I see, and apply Bayesian methods to optimize the processes that result.

During my PhD, I developed non-invasive flow and concentration measurement techniques for electrodialysis stacks and sterile filtration modules. I also supervised student teams, mentored junior researchers, and taught courses on process engineering fundamentals.

I'm currently exploring new challenges — in pharma/biotech process development, medical imaging research, simulation engineering, or the broader field of applied measurement science. I'm happiest when I can combine rigorous experiments with computational modeling to solve real problems.
