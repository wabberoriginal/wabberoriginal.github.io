Hobbies

What I do when I'm not thinking about flow fields and membrane systems.

🛶

Canoe Polo

I play canoe polo with my club — a sport that's equal parts kayaking, water polo, and controlled chaos. It's fast, physical, and requires the kind of spatial awareness that my day job in fluid dynamics probably helps with. I've competed in tournaments across Germany and written the occasional match report for our club newsletter.

🏔

Hiking

Whether it's a day hike in the Eifel or something longer in the Alps, I find that walking through landscapes is one of the best ways to think clearly — or to stop thinking entirely. Both are valuable.

🍳

Cooking

I cook vegetarian — sometimes vegan — and I like to go elaborate. Multi-course dinners, fermentation experiments, optimizing plant protein combinations, or just making a really good dal from scratch. I'm genuinely interested in the nutritional science behind what I cook, from omega-3 fatty acid sources to creatine biochemistry on a meat-free diet.

♟

Chess & Games

I play chess regularly — mostly online, occasionally over a real board. I also enjoy card games like Skat and the occasional deep dive into video games. Baldur's Gate 3 consumed a significant portion of my free time, and I'm not even sorry about it.

🤝

Volunteering

Community work has been a constant thread through my life. I've held leadership roles in youth organizations (KLJB, Scouts), served on a choir board, and been involved with the Quickborn-Arbeitskreis at Burg Rothenfels. I'm also a member of a student fraternity (Studentenverbindung), where I've stayed active in organizational and mentoring roles. These experiences shaped how I think about teamwork, responsibility, and communication just as much as any research project.

🖨

3D Printing

I tinker with 3D printing — mostly functional parts, custom lab fixtures, and the occasional gift. There's something satisfying about going from a CAD sketch to a physical object in an afternoon.
